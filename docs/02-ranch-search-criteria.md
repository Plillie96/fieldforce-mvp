# 02 — Ranch Search Criteria

The purpose of this document is to make "the right ranch" an objective test
instead of a gut feeling. Every candidate in
[`research/ranch-tracker.md`](../research/ranch-tracker.md) gets scored against it.

## The single most important number: carrying capacity

A ranch's value to a beef operation is not its acreage — it's how many animals
it can feed. This is the **stocking rate**, measured in acres per **animal unit
(AU)** (one AU ≈ a 1,000 lb cow with calf).

Stocking rate varies enormously by region and rainfall:

| Region type | Rough acres per AU | Notes |
|-------------|--------------------|-------|
| Humid Southeast / irrigated pasture | 1.5 – 4 | High productivity, high land cost |
| Midwest / mixed-grass | 3 – 8 | Balance of cost and capacity |
| Interior West rangeland | 15 – 40 | Cheap per acre, needs a *lot* of it |
| Arid Southwest / high desert | 40 – 100+ | Deceptively cheap; often can't support a herd |

**Implication:** a "cheap" 2,000-acre ranch in the high desert may carry fewer
cattle than an expensive 300-acre irrigated place. Always underwrite on
**cost per animal unit of sustainable carrying capacity**, not cost per acre.

> Target for the assumed 150-head herd: enough deeded + leasable AU to run the
> herd through a normal-to-dry year *without* overgrazing. Ask the seller and
> the local NRCS office for the historical stocking rate — don't take the
> listing's number at face value.

## Hard requirements (a "no" on any of these kills the deal)

- **Reliable water.** Year-round stock water — wells, springs, live creek, or
  senior water rights. In the West, **verify water rights are deeded and
  transferable**, not just that water is physically present. No water, no ranch.
- **Legal access.** Deeded road access, not a handshake easement across a
  neighbor's land. Confirm in the title.
- **Clear title & known encumbrances.** Existing leases, easements, mineral
  severance, conservation easements — all identified before offer.
- **Zoning / ag use permitted.** Livestock operation and (ideally) on-farm sales
  allowed under local zoning.

## Scored criteria (weighted scorecard)

Score each 1–5; multiply by weight; a candidate needs a weighted total above
threshold to advance to due diligence.

| Criterion | Weight | What "5" looks like |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Carrying capacity per dollar | 25% | Ample sustainable AU at a defensible $/AU |
| Water security | 20% | Multiple year-round sources + deeded rights |
| Existing infrastructure | 15% | Sound fencing, corrals, barn, handling facilities, home |
| Forage quality & soil | 10% | Productive native pasture or improvable ground |
| Proximity to processing | 10% | USDA-inspected plant within a workable haul (< ~3 hrs) |
| Proximity to logistics/customers | 8% | Reasonable access to shipping and metro demand |
| Climate & drought risk | 7% | Stable rainfall history, low catastrophic-drought exposure |
| Expansion / lease-up potential | 5% | Adjacent leasable ground to scale the herd |

## Infrastructure checklist (affects cost to operate on day one)

- [ ] Perimeter and cross-fencing condition (rebuilding fence is $$$/mile)
- [ ] Livestock water distribution (tanks, pipelines, troughs)
- [ ] Corrals / squeeze chute / loading facilities
- [ ] Barn / hay storage / equipment shelter
- [ ] Habitable residence and utilities (power, septic, internet — internet
      matters for a D2C business)
- [ ] Hay ground or ability to buy winter feed locally

## Red flags

- Listed stocking rate that's far above the regional norm (overgrazing risk).
- "Water available" with no documented rights in a prior-appropriation state.
- Landlocked parcel or access "by easement" that isn't recorded.
- Severed mineral rights with active leasing — surface disruption risk.
- Priced on "recreational" or "development" comps rather than ag productivity
  (you'll overpay relative to what cattle can earn).

## Where to source candidates

- Ag/ranch brokerages: Hall and Hall, Whitetail Properties, Mason & Morse,
  Fay Ranches, LandLeader, United Country.
- Listing aggregators: Lands of America, LandWatch, Land And Farm, Ranch.com.
- Off-market: local county extension offices, auction notices, FSA, estate
  sales, and word of mouth at regional cattle auctions.
- USDA FSA and land-link programs (retiring-rancher succession).

Log every serious candidate in the tracker with its scorecard.
