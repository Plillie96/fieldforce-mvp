# Undiscovered Vein Targets — Harquahala & Little Harquahala Mountains, West-Central Arizona

**A desktop exploration-targeting review built from public data**
La Paz & Maricopa Counties, Arizona · Compiled 2026-06-08

---

## 0. What this is (and isn't)

This is a **desktop synthesis of the public record** — USGS Mineral Resources Data
System (MRDS), USGS Wilderness Study Area mineral appraisals, Arizona Geological
Survey (AZGS) geologic maps, and the published detachment-fault / orogenic-gold
deposit-model literature. No proprietary data was used. Every "target" below is a
**desktop-generated lead** — a place where structure, historic workings, and
geochemistry line up favorably — **not** a drill-confirmed discovery. All of it
requires ground-truthing. Confidence is stated explicitly throughout.

Three framing corrections that matter before reading on:

1. **District naming in the public databases is unreliable.** Online aggregators
   (Mindat, The Diggings, Western Mining History) lump two physically distinct
   ranges under "Harquahala District." The famous **Bonanza / Harqua Hala mine and
   the Golden Eagle mine are physically in the *Little* Harquahala Mountains**
   (Martin Peak area, ~T4N R13W), hosted in a Mesozoic thrust stack. The
   **Socorro, San Marcos, and Hidden Treasure mines are in the *main* Harquahala
   range** (Socorro Peak / Wenden side) covered by USGS Bulletin 1701-C. Note also
   that Bulletin 1701-C assigns Bonanza/Socorro/Hercules to the **"Ellsworth"
   metallic district** — yet another label for the same ground. **Trust MRDS
   township/range + lat-long, not the text district label.**

2. **The detachment fault is the Eagle Eye, not the Bullard.** The Harquahala
   metamorphic core complex is bounded on its NE side by the **Eagle Eye (Peak)
   detachment fault**. The Bullard detachment bounds the *adjacent Harcuvar*
   Mountains. They are kinematically the same NE-directed Miocene system
   (Buckskin–Rawhide–Bullard–Eagle Eye), ~44 ± 2 km total slip, active ~21–14 Ma.

3. **The age and type of the Bonanza/Harquahala gold ore is genuinely unresolved
   in the literature** — it could be Late Cretaceous–Laramide *mesothermal /
   orogenic* gold-quartz, or mid-Tertiary *detachment-related*, or a Tertiary
   overprint on a Mesozoic system. This ambiguity is the single most important
   caveat for any model applied here, and it actually *doubles* the target set
   (two deposit styles to chase).

**Data-quality cautions:** historic production figures vary widely by source
(district gold endowment is quoted anywhere from ~94,000 to ~200,000 oz). There is
**no independent NI 43-101 technical report** anywhere in either district. Several
secondary pages (Mindat, The Diggings) blocked automated fetch, so some fine detail
is from search snippets. Exact wilderness-boundary geometry was not retrieved at
parcel precision and should be checked against the BLM Arizona Wilderness map before
any field or staking decision.

## 1. Geologic framework — two overprinting mineral systems

The ranges expose a **footwall (lower plate) of Proterozoic basement and Mesozoic
thrust sheets**, structurally overlain across the Eagle Eye detachment by an
**upper plate of tilted mid-Tertiary volcanic and sedimentary rocks**. Two
metallogenic systems are superimposed:

**(A) Mesozoic–Laramide mesothermal / orogenic gold-quartz veins** — the historic
bonanza producers.
- Host: strongly deformed, thrust-stacked Paleozoic carbonate–quartzite and
  Mesozoic clastic rocks, intruded by an elongate **quartz monzonite** body and by
  the Late Cretaceous–early Tertiary **muscovite-garnet granite of Browns Canyon**.
- The Little Harquahala thrust stack (AZGS OFR-85-09, Spencer–Richard–Reynolds
  1985) is three allochthons — **Harquar plate** (Jurassic volcanics + McCoy Mtns
  Fm clastics) at the base, **Hercules plate** (crystalline) over the Hercules
  thrust, **Centennial plate** (Precambrian + Paleozoic + Mesozoic sediments) over
  the Centennial thrust. This places **Paleozoic carbonate structurally over
  Mesozoic clastics** — a strongly reactive, fluid-focusing architecture.
- Ore style: brecciated, lenticular **quartz–jasper veins** along shear/fault
  zones; pockety high-grade gold shoots; oxidized cap (iron oxides + calcite +
  gypsum) passing **downward into auriferous pyrite ± Cu-Pb sulfides**.
- Pathfinders: Au, Ag, As, ±Sb, Te, W (W where veins approach Paleozoic marble).

**(B) Mid-Tertiary detachment-fault-related Cu-Au-Fe-Mn-Ba mineralization** —
under-recognized and under-tested in these ranges, but a *proven producer* in the
neighboring Harcuvar (Bullard mine: 614,000 lb Cu + 3,300 oz Au) and
Buckskin-Rawhide (~24 M lb Mn) ranges.
- Position: in/along the Eagle Eye detachment, in upper-plate breccias, and in
  footwall fractures just below the detachment.
- Ore/gangue: specular & earthy **hematite, chrysocolla, malachite, gold, silver**;
  gangue quartz–calcite–**barite–fluorite–Mn-oxide**; sulfides (pyrite,
  chalcopyrite) survive below the oxide cap.
- Alteration: chlorite–epidote proximal/deep; regional **K-metasomatism**
  (K-feldspar/adularia, 8–12 wt% K₂O at the Bullard mine) in upper-plate volcanics.
- **Diagnostic geochemistry:** Cu-Au-Ag-Ba-F-Mn-Zn-Pb enriched, while **As, Sb, Hg,
  Tl are LOW (background)** — the low-As/Sb signature distinguishes detachment ore
  from epithermal/Carlin systems. Metal zonation: base-metal-rich *near* the
  detachment, precious-metal-rich *away* from it.

*The two vein families are the master pattern:* the **big producers sit on
relatively FLAT structures** (Bonanza, Socorro), while the **many smaller veins
follow NW-striking HIGH-ANGLE faults** (Sunset Canyon, Dushey Canyon, Blue Tank
Canyon) adjacent to a NW-trending **mid-Tertiary gabbro dike swarm**. Intersections
of these two families are the highest-priority structural traps (see Target 6).

## 3. The targets

Ranked by a blend of geological merit, strength of supporting public evidence, and
testability. Each lists the play, the evidence, how to test it, land status, and
confidence.

### Target 1 — Concealed pluton & its vein/skarn aureole, Blue Tank Canyon / southern Harquahala range  ★ highest-value documented anomaly
- **Play:** A coincident **aeromagnetic high + gravity high** indicates a strongly
  magnetic, dense body — interpreted as a mafic (gabbroic) **pluton ~50 mi² in
  area, buried ≤2.5 mi (≤4 km) deep**, centered near Blue Tank. It is spatially
  tied to (a) the highest Cu grades in the WSA (Area 12 "Northwest": **2.29% Cu**,
  ~0.14 oz/t Au) and (b) the NW gabbro dike swarm that the gold veins parallel. The
  pluton is best read as the **heat/metal engine**: chase fracture-controlled and
  contact (skarn/replacement) Au-Ag-Cu around its concealed apex and margins, not
  the barren interior of a gabbro.
- **Evidence:** USGS Bulletin 1701-C geophysics (Figs. 4–5); USGS explicitly
  declined to rate it *only because depth/grade are unknown* and recommended
  **detailed gravity + helicopter magnetics** to characterize it. Never drilled.
- **Test:** high-resolution heli-mag + gravity inversion to map the apex and dike
  feeders → CSAMT/IP over the apex and along dike-fault intersections → deep
  drilling.
- **Land status:** straddles the wilderness boundary; the **south flank is open
  BLM**. Confirm the line before staking.
- **Confidence:** the geophysical anomaly is **high**; "economic ore" is
  **speculative/medium** — the value is that this is the best-defined, never-tested
  blind target in the district.

### Target 2 — Eagle Eye detachment corridor, west of Browns Canyon Wash (Areas 5 & 8)  ★ best detachment-style play
- **Play:** Concealed **detachment-style Au-Ag-Mn-Cu** under thin gravel along the
  Eagle Eye detachment, directly analogous to the producing Bullard / Buckskin-
  Rawhide systems one range NE.
- **Evidence:** Hematite–chrysocolla–malachite in upper-plate rocks here, **plus the
  only Zn (1,500 ppm), Pb (4,000 ppm) and fluorite anomalies in the entire WSA** —
  a textbook detachment geochemical fingerprint. Bulletin 1701-C rates these areas
  "unknown potential" **solely because stream-sediment coverage is absent**, and
  states the ground "could be penetrated by shallow drill holes or explored by use
  of geophysical techniques."
- **Test:** map the detachment + upper-plate breccias → soil/rock geochem for
  Cu-Au-Ag-Ba-F-Mn-Zn-Pb and **confirm As-Sb-Hg are low** (validates the model) →
  ground magnetics for hematite bodies + CSAMT/resistivity to trace silicified/oxide
  zones and the detachment surface beneath gravel → **shallow RC drilling** through
  the cover (USGS's own recommendation).
- **Land status:** much of Area 5/8 sits in/near the former WSA → verify
  wilderness-withdrawal line carefully; favorable strike likely continues onto open
  ground.
- **Confidence:** **medium-high** as a valid, correctly-typed target; grade and size
  unknown.

### Target 3 — Down-plunge sulfide continuation of the Bonanza–Golden Eagle shoots (Little Harquahala)
- **Play:** Historic mining took the **oxidized bonanza ore that was "mined out"
  near surface by ~1895–1907**, from a shaft only **600 ft** deep. Ore is
  documented to pass **downward into auriferous pyrite (primary sulfide)** — i.e.,
  the primary hypogene system below the oxide zone was **barely tested**. Two
  parallel shoots exist (E shoot N–S/45°W; W shoot 45°E, *the W shoot was the
  richer*).
- **Test:** re-log surviving workings and old assay sheets → **deep IP/chargeability**
  to image the pyritic shoot below the historic levels and along its plunge →
  twin/deepen drilling under the W shoot and test the dilational down-dip flexures
  where the flat shear cuts competent quartzite.
- **Land status:** **actively claimed/operated (RMC Mining Development since 2023;
  Tombstone/Goldrock before)** — *not* open ground. The geologic target is real but
  belongs to the current operator; this is a partnership/data-room lead, not a
  staking lead.
- **Confidence:** **medium-high** that hypogene ore continues (normal for a bonanza
  oxide deposit); continuity is uncertain because of pervasive **post-mineral
  faulting** in the thrust stack.

### Target 4 — SE strike extension of Arrastre Gulch (Area 9) — the highest gold grade sampled in the WSA
- **Play:** Area 9 returned the **highest gold grade in the USBM WSA sampling,
  0.23 oz/t Au**. Its continuity **to the SE was never sampled because it leaves the
  study area onto open ground.**
- **Test:** extend geologic mapping + soil/rock geochem SE onto open BLM along the
  vein/shear trend; trench and channel-sample.
- **Land status:** the untested SE extension is **open BLM** — favorable.
- **Confidence:** **medium** — a clear, cheap, near-surface follow-up of a known
  high grade.

### Target 5 — Tungsten (±Au-Ag) skarn/replacement in concealed Paleozoic marble beneath the Harquahala thrust (Area 13, White Marble → Arrastre Gulch)
- **Play:** W anomalies are **restricted to the Paleozoic-floored southern WSA**,
  in skarn/replacement bodies in **marble beneath the Harquahala thrust** — an
  untested **replacement** target distinct from the quartz veins, plausibly tied to
  the same concealed pluton (Target 1).
- **Test:** map thrust geometry → soil geochem W-Au-As-Cu → gravity/mag to locate
  buried marble + causative intrusive contact → drill the favorable
  marble/intrusive interface.
- **Land status:** check boundary; partly WSA-derived.
- **Confidence:** **medium**; different commodity, but a real and unexplored
  replacement style.

### Target 6 — Structural-intersection criterion: NW high-angle faults × flat thrust/detachment surfaces
- **Play:** This is a **targeting rule** more than a single point. The richest
  historic ore (Bonanza, Socorro) sits on **flat** structures; the dike-related
  veins sit on **NW high-angle faults** (Sunset/Dushey/Blue Tank Canyon). Where the
  two families **intersect** — especially in competent quartzite and near the gabbro
  dike swarm above the concealed pluton apex — you get the dilational, fluid-focusing
  traps that make ore shoots. Hercules' "faulted-off, discontinuous" ore says the
  *same faults that trap ore also offset it* → offset blocks are untested.
- **Test:** build a GIS structural model from AZGS OFR-85-09, OFR-94-17 (Salome
  1:100k), and the Bulletin 1701-C plate; rank fault-fault and fault-dike
  intersections; prioritize those coincident with the magnetic/gravity apex and with
  detachment geochemistry.
- **Confidence:** **conceptual / medium** — the strongest *organizing principle* for
  prioritizing everything above.

## 4. Geochemical & geophysical vectoring cheat-sheet

**To find concealed *mesothermal* Au-quartz veins (Targets 3, 4, partly 1/5):**
- Geochem: **Au, Ag, As, ±Sb, Te**; **W** where veins near Paleozoic marble.
- Geophysics: **IP/chargeability** for pyritic shoots at depth; structural targeting
  on shear/fault/thrust intersections; magnetics to map the dike swarm and pluton.

**To find concealed *detachment* Cu-Au-Mn-Ba veins (Targets 2, and the
chrysocolla-Mn signature already seen at Hidden Treasure):**
- Geochem (positive): **Cu, Au, Ag, Ba, F, Mn, Zn, Pb**. Geochem (diagnostic
  negative): **As, Sb, Hg, Tl LOW** — confirms detachment vs. epithermal.
- Alteration to map: **specular hematite, chrysocolla/malachite, barite, fluorite,
  Mn-oxides, jasperoid/silicification**; **K-feldspar/adularia** halo; chlorite-
  epidote at the proximal/deep zone.
- Geophysics: **magnetics** (massive hematite = mag high; magnetite *destruction* in
  altered zones), **resistivity** high over silicified/oxide ore + resistivity
  contrast across the detachment, **IP** for surviving sulfides below the oxide cap,
  **radiometric K** for K-metasomatism, **gravity** for detachment geometry and the
  pluton.
- Zonation rule: base-metal-rich *near* the detachment, precious-metal-rich *away*.

## 5. Land status (verify before any field action)

- **Harquahala Mountains Wilderness** — 22,880 ac (La Paz + Maricopa Co.),
  designated by the **Arizona Desert Wilderness Act of 1990**, BLM Hassayampa Field
  Office. **Big Horn Mountains Wilderness** — 20,600 ac, abuts to the SE.
- Designated wilderness is **withdrawn from mineral location, entry, patent, and
  leasing, subject to valid existing rights** (Wilderness Act). **Ground outside the
  wilderness boundary remains open to claim-staking.**
- The **Little Harquahala range (Bonanza/Golden Eagle) is NOT wilderness** — open
  BLM, actively claimed and operated.
- Several of the best USGS targets (Areas 2/9/11/12 and the buried pluton)
  **straddle the wilderness line**, and the report repeatedly notes favorable strike
  continues *outside* the protected core onto open ground.
- **Action item:** overlay the BLM Arizona Wilderness/WSA boundary and current
  MLRS/LR2000 claim footprints on the target coordinates above before committing.

## 7. Bottom line

If I had to spend the first dollars: **(1)** re-process the existing aeromag/gravity
over the **Blue Tank concealed pluton** and run **shallow drilling on the Eagle Eye
detachment corridor west of Browns Canyon Wash** (Target 2) — it's the cheapest test
of the most clearly-typed, USGS-endorsed blind target; **(2)** in parallel, walk the
**SE open-ground extension of Arrastre Gulch** (Target 4, highest sampled Au, open
BLM); **(3)** treat the **Bonanza down-plunge sulfide** (Target 3) as a
partnership/data-room play with the current operator, since the ground isn't open.
Everything hangs on resolving the **ore-age question** — get one good geochronology
sample (vein white-mica or alteration K-feldspar) and you'll know whether you're
chasing a Laramide orogenic system, a Miocene detachment system, or both.
